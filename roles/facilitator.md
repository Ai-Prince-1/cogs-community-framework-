# Facilitator Playbook

**Your job in one sentence:** Hold the time and the space.

---

## Before the Gathering

- Confirm the gathering time and send a brief reminder to the group
- Review last week's commitments from the group thread
- Mentally prepare to hold neutral, non-judgmental space

---

## During The Open (10 min)

**Opening line:**
> "Let's begin. One sentence each — how are you arriving today?"

- **No advice. No responses. Just hold space and listen.**
- Keep it to one sentence per person. Time it.
- If someone goes longer than one sentence, gently redirect: "One sentence, and we'll come back to you if there's time."
- Do NOT respond to what people share. Hearing is enough.
- If the room feels heavy, acknowledge it simply: "I hear we're all carrying a lot today."

---

## During The Share (45 min)

**Opening:**
> "[Name], would you like to start? Tell us about your week in [domains]."

- Watch the clock. This is non-negotiable.
- Redistribute time gently if someone runs long: "Thanks for that. We need to give [Name] time too. One more minute?"
- Encourage honest reporting: tries, wins, failures, learnings — all of it matters
- If someone is vague, push back gently: "What did that look like specifically?"
- If advice starts to flow, redirect: "Let's let them finish first."
- Your job is to create space for truth, not to fix or interpret

---

## During The Commit (15 min)

**Opening:**
> "What is your specific action for this week?"

- Ask each person the same question
- Listen for specificity. Push back on vague intentions:
  - ❌ "I'll try to be healthier"
  - ✅ "I will walk 30 minutes on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday"
- If it's not concrete enough, ask: "Can you make that more specific? What exactly will you do, and when?"
- Make sure every person names something before moving on

---

## At The Hand-Off (Close)

**Closing line:**
> "Thank you all. [Name] will facilitate next week."

- Name the next Facilitator explicitly. Don't let it go unnamed.
- End on time. Every time. This is the promise.
- If you're running over, it's okay to call it: "We're at 70 minutes. [Name] will pick this up next week."

---

## If Something Goes Sideways

- Name it simply and move on: "I notice we're giving a lot of time to one person. Let's redistribute."
- If emotions are high, slow down: "Let's pause for a moment. What do people need right now?"
- If conflict emerges, hold it gently: "This is important. Let's come back to it after gathering."
- Remember: You're not here to solve problems. You're here to hold the structure.

---

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Interpret, evaluate, or comment on what members share
- ❌ Give advice unless specifically asked in The Share
- ❌ Dominate the conversation or share your own story
- ❌ Skip The Hand-Off — this is how rotation stays alive
- ❌ Let the gathering run over without addressing it
- ❌ Make exceptions to the structure without group consent

---

## First-Time Facilitator Guidance

- **Nervous is normal.** The structure does most of the work for you.
- **Your job is not to be impressive.** It is to hold the time and the space.
- **The group will support you.** That's the whole point.
- **If you mess up, say so.** "I lost track of time — can we reset?" is fine.
