# Steward Playbook

**Your job in one sentence:** Keep the money transparent and clean.

---

## Core Principles

- **Radical transparency.** Every member can see every transaction at any time.
- **No personal benefit.** You cannot profit from Circle resources.
- **Full Circle consent.** Every financial decision is made by the full group, not by you alone.
- **You execute, you don't decide.** Your role is to track and implement, not to choose how money is spent.

---

## Maintaining the Ledger

**Create and maintain one simple, transparent ledger that shows:**
- Date of transaction
- What was purchased or contributed
- Amount
- Who contributed or spent
- What it was for
- Current balance

**Example:**
```
| Date | Description | Amount | Type | Balance |
|------|-------------|--------|------|----------|
| 6/1  | Sarah contribution | +$50 | Deposit | $50 |
| 6/5  | Snacks for gathering | -$12 | Expense | $38 |
| 6/8  | Marcus contribution | +$30 | Deposit | $68 |
```

**Update the ledger within 24 hours of any transaction.**

---

## Access and Reporting

- **Every member has read access at all times. No exceptions.**
- Store the ledger in at least two accessible places (shared Google Sheet + printed copy + email backup)
- Report on the ledger at any gathering where resources are relevant
- Answer questions about spending anytime, not just at gatherings
- If someone asks about a transaction, explain it immediately

---

## Making Financial Decisions

**Never spend shared resources without full Circle consent.**

**Process:**
1. A need or idea arises
2. Bring it to the Circle: "We have $68. Do we want to spend $15 on [X]?"
3. Get explicit agreement from everyone
4. Document the decision in writing
5. Update the ledger

**Example of bad: "We need snacks. I'll just buy them."**
**Example of good: "Should we spend $20 from our pool on snacks for the next gathering? Yes? Great, I'll pick them up and update the ledger."**

---

## At Season End

**Present a final ledger summary at the Retrospective that shows:**
- Total contributed
- Total spent
- Current balance
- What the money was used for
- Any major decisions or challenges

**Example:**
```
Season X Financial Summary
- Total Contributions: $148
- Total Expenses: $75
- Remaining Balance: $73
- Spent on: Venue rental ($45), Snacks ($20), Materials ($10)
- Decision needed: What happens to the remaining $73?
```

---

## Handing Off to Your Successor

- Give them the complete, organized ledger with all documentation
- Walk them through every transaction so they understand context
- Show them where it's stored and who has access
- Remind them: this is a trust role, not a power role
- Answer their questions

---

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't spend money without asking first
- ❌ Don't keep the ledger secret or hard to access
- ❌ Don't make financial decisions alone
- ❌ Don't receive personal benefit from Circle assets
- ❌ Don't wait to update the ledger — do it within 24 hours
- ❌ Don't make exceptions to the transparency rule

---

## If Something Is Unclear

- Ask the Circle immediately
- If you're unsure whether something should be spent, bring it to the group first
- If there's a financial concern or problem, flag it to the full Circle right away
- Don't sit on financial decisions — transparency requires immediacy
