# Bridge Playbook

**Your job in one sentence:** Carry information both ways. Commit nothing unilaterally.

---

## Your Role at a Glance

You are the representative of your Circle at the Mesh. You:
- Attend Mesh coordination gatherings
- Bring information back to your Circle
- Represent your Circle's values and needs
- Never commit your Circle to anything without full Circle consent

---

## Before Mesh Meetings

- **Check with your Circle:** "Is there anything the Mesh should know about us?"
- **Get Circle input on Mesh proposals:** "The Mesh is discussing [X]. What's our take?"
- **Review the distributed registry:** Make sure your Circle's entry is current
- **Know your boundaries:** What CAN you decide? What MUST come back to the Circle?

---

## At Mesh Meetings

**You have voice. You do NOT have a vote that binds your Circle.**

- Listen to what other Circles are doing
- Share what your Circle is learning and experiencing
- Participate in discussions
- Ask clarifying questions
- If you don't know your Circle's position on something, SAY SO: "I need to check with my Circle on that"
- Do NOT agree to anything on behalf of your Circle

**Example of bad:** "Our Circle will volunteer for that project."
**Example of good:** "That sounds interesting. Let me bring it back to my Circle and we'll decide together."

---

## Reporting Back to Your Circle

**After every Mesh meeting, report to your Circle at the next gathering.**

Share:
- What other Circles are working on
- What the Mesh discussed
- Any proposals or ideas that affect your Circle
- Any conflicts or opportunities
- What your Circle needs to decide

**Example report:**
```
Mesh Update:
- Three Circles are planning a joint learning event on financial literacy
- The Mesh is updating the registry — can we confirm our contact info?
- There's a request for mediation from Circle B — does anyone want to volunteer?
- The Mesh is meeting again in 6 weeks at [location]
```

---

## Handling Mesh Proposals

**If the Mesh proposes something affecting your Circle:**

1. **Listen fully.** Don't decide on the spot.
2. **Say: "Let me bring this back to my Circle."**
3. **Take it to the next Circle gathering.**
4. **Get Circle consent** (or simple agreement) on whether you proceed
5. **Report back to the Mesh** at the next meeting

**Key rule:** No Mesh proposal binds your Circle without full Circle agreement.

---

## Maintaining the Mesh Registry

- **Keep your Circle's entry current** in the distributed registry
- Make sure contact info is accurate
- Update it if your Circle's Facilitator changes or your Mesh location shifts
- Ensure the registry is stored in multiple places (it should be)
- Help other Circles access the registry if they need it

---

## If You Can't Attend a Mesh Meeting

- **Arrange a proxy.** Find someone in your Circle to represent you.
- **Brief them fully:** Share what they need to know, what decisions might come up, your Circle's positions
- **Give them the same boundaries:** They can listen and share, but cannot commit the Circle
- **Get a report** when they return

---

## Flagging Cross-Circle Issues

If you see something affecting multiple Circles:
- Bring it to the Mesh
- Suggest it be addressed at a Mesh coordination meeting
- Help broker connection between Circles if needed

---

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't commit your Circle to anything without full Circle consent
- ❌ Don't make decisions on behalf of your Circle
- ❌ Don't skip reporting back to your Circle
- ❌ Don't assume you know what your Circle thinks about something
- ❌ Don't let the Mesh override your Circle's decisions
- ❌ Don't keep important information from your Circle

---

## Remember

- **You have no special authority.** You're a messenger and listener.
- **Your Circle always decides.** You report back and facilitate their choice.
- **The Mesh coordinates, it doesn't govern.** You protect that boundary.
- **Transparency is everything.** What happens at Mesh, your Circle knows.
