# Seeding Ceremony: How to Split a Circle

When a Circle grows large or reaches a natural inflection point, it may be time to split and seed new Circles. This ceremony creates intentional new communities while honoring the original.

## When to Split

- Circle exceeds 12-15 active members
- Multiple sub-groups naturally form
- Geographic distribution makes regular gathering difficult
- Original mission and new direction diverge
- Organic request from members

## The Seeding Ceremony (90-120 minutes)

### Opening (15 min)
- Gather the full Circle
- Acknowledge what you've built together
- Frame the split as growth, not dissolution
- Set the intention: "We are seeding new life"

### Reflecting on Legacy (20 min)
- What has this Circle meant?
- What values or practices do we want to carry forward?
- What have we learned?
- What should new Circles inherit?

### Self-Selection into New Circles (20 min)
- Present the proposed new Circle(s) and their focus
- Explain the criteria for each
- Allow people to self-select into new groups
- Keep the original Circle intact if some wish to continue

### Bridge Roles (15 min)
- Assign 1-2 people to "bridge" between groups
- Their role is to maintain connection and learning flow
- Schedule regular inter-Circle gatherings
- Share knowledge and practices

### Commissioning (15 min)
- Each new Circle receives a blessing or affirmation
- Original Circle members offer wisdom or encouragement
- Acknowledge the courage to begin something new

### Logistics (15 min)
- Confirm meeting times and locations
- Clarify shared resources
- Establish communication channels
- Plan first independent meeting

### Closing (10 min)
- Gratitude circle
- Reaffirm commitment to the broader network
- Celebrate growth

## Post-Split Practices

- Monthly inter-Circle gatherings for first season
- Quarterly all-hands assembly for all Circles
- Shared practices and resource library
- Peer support and mentoring
- Annual reunion celebration
