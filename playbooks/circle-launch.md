# Launch a Circle

**Start your first COGS Circle in one evening.**

The Circle of Good Stewards (COGS) is a lightweight, non-doctrinal framework for small groups of people who want to grow together — physically, financially, emotionally, and relationally — with honesty and accountability.

You don’t need permission, money, or special tools. Just 3–7 people who trust each other enough to show up honestly.

---

## Quick Start Checklist

- [ ] Identify 3–6 founding members (friends, neighbors, colleagues, family)
- [ ] Have an informal conversation with them
- [ ] Schedule your first **Season Opening** (90–120 minutes)
- [ ] Set up a shared group chat (Signal, WhatsApp, etc.)
- [ ] Share the [Manifesto](../manifesto.md) and this Handbook

---

## Step-by-Step: How to Launch

### 1. Find Your People

**Ideal size**: 4–6 people (minimum 3, maximum 7).

**Look for people who**:
- Show up when they say they will
- Are willing to be honest about their real life
- Can disagree without withdrawing
- Are curious and open to learning

**Avoid (for the founding group)**:
- Chronic last-minute cancelers
- People who need to dominate every conversation
- Anyone in acute unmanaged crisis

### 2. Have the First Conversation

Invite them informally. Use this script (adapt as needed):

> "I’m thinking of starting a small group — 4–6 people meeting weekly for about 70 minutes.  
> Each season we pick 2–3 areas of life to focus on (health, money, relationships, etc.).  
> We share what we’re trying, what’s working, what isn’t, and hold each other accountable in a light, honest way.  
> No dogma, no guru, no weird stuff — just real people trying to get better together.  
> Interested?"

Give everyone space to ask questions and opt in genuinely.

### 3. Hold Your First Season Opening (Week 1)

This is the foundational meeting. Plan for **90–120 minutes**.

#### Season Opening Agenda

| Time     | Activity                          |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| 15 min   | Check-in round                    |
| 20 min   | Choose 2–3 focus domains          |
| 20 min   | Each person shares a personal intention |
| 15 min   | Confirm weekly Facilitator rotation |
| 10 min   | Optional: Resources & outward support |
| 10 min   | Close + confirm next meeting time |

**Recommended domains** (pick 2–3):
- Financial Literacy
- Physical Health
- Mental & Emotional Health
- Relationships
- Philosophy & Ethics
- Creativity & Expression
- Career & Purpose
- etc. (see full list in Handbook)

### 4. Start the Weekly Rhythm (70 minutes)

Every week:
- **10 min** – The Open (one-sentence check-in, listening only)
- **45 min** – The Share (progress on domains + last week’s commitments)
- **15 min** – The Commit (specific, measurable action for next week)
- Hand-off to next Facilitator

After the meeting, everyone posts their commitment in the group chat.

---

## First-Time Tips

- **Start small and consistent** — 70 minutes sharp is better than ambitious and flaky.
- **In-person is best**, but hybrid/remote works if cameras stay on.
- **Facilitation rotates** from week one — no permanent leader.
- **Be kind but honest** — the power is in the practice, not perfection.
- Review the [Red Flags](../handbook.md#section-8--anti-cult-and-self-correction-protocols) early so everyone knows the guardrails.

---

## Next Steps After Launch

- Complete your first full 12-week Season
- Run a proper **Season Retrospective** at Week 12
- Decide whether to stay at Level 1 or activate any Level 2 modules
- Consider joining or forming a Mesh with other Circles
