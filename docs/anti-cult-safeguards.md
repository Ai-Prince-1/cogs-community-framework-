# Anti-Cult Safeguards

This document outlines the principles and practices that keep COGS grounded and prevent it from becoming a cult or dogmatic institution.

## Core Principles

### 1. Transparency & Accountability
- All decisions are documented and accessible
- Leadership is rotational and accountable
- Financial records are public
- Conflicts of interest are disclosed

### 2. Decentralization
- No single leader or central authority
- Decisions are made by consensus or democratic vote
- Local circles have autonomy
- Diversity of thought is encouraged

### 3. Exit Rights
- Anyone can leave at any time, no questions asked
- No shunning or ostracization of departing members
- Sharing of knowledge is encouraged upon exit
- No financial penalties for leaving

### 4. Critical Thinking
- Questions and disagreement are welcomed
- The framework is open to critique and improvement
- No doctrine is sacred or unchangeable
- Evidence-based practices are preferred over dogma

### 5. Anti-Coercion
- No coercive recruitment tactics
- Participation is voluntary
- Decisions cannot be made under pressure or emotional manipulation
- Informed consent is required for all commitments

### 6. Balanced Power
- Resource distribution prevents wealth concentration
- Leadership roles rotate regularly
- Minority voices are protected
- Decisions require broad agreement

## Red Flags to Avoid

- 🚫 Charismatic leader figures
- 🚫 Us vs. them mentality
- 🚫 Pressure to recruit or proselytize
- 🚫 Isolation from outside communities
- 🚫 Financial exploitation
- 🚫 Punishment for questioning leadership
- 🚫 Required beliefs or practices
- 🚫 Secrecy or hidden agendas

## Continuous Vigilance

Every Circle should regularly review these safeguards and discuss:
- Are we staying true to these principles?
- Where might we be drifting?
- What early warning signs should we watch for?
- How can we course-correct?
